Executive Summary
Forbes publishes roughly 400 pieces of content per day across web, newsletters, and social. With a newsroom of approximately 150 editorial staff, that output requires significant operational overhead — and the pressure to increase volume while maintaining quality is not going away.
 
This document proposes the Forbes AI Content Generation Platform ("Quill"): an internal, editorial-grade AI tool designed to reduce the time journalists spend on repeatable, low-value tasks, so they can spend more of it on the reporting that only they can do.
 
This is not a replacement for journalists. It is — deliberately and specifically — an augmentation layer. Quill sits inside the existing editorial workflow and handles four distinct jobs: research summarization, SEO metadata generation, social distribution copy, and newsletter draft assembly. It does not write articles. That decision is explained in Section 3.

1. The Problem We Are Solving
Before designing any solution, we spent three weeks inside the existing workflow. We interviewed (Synthetic) 14 editors, reporters, and SEO specialists across Forbes' Business, Technology, and Finance verticals. We observed three full news cycles. What we found was consistent and, in places, surprising.	

1.2  What Journalists Actually Said
Three quotes from user interviews that shaped this PRD:
 
"I spend 45 minutes every morning just reading through earnings reports and pulling out the numbers that matter. It's not writing. It's just reading fast." — Senior Reporter, Finance
 
"Our SEO team sends back metadata requests after every single story. It breaks my flow completely. There's no reason a human should be writing description tags." — Staff Writer, Technology
 
"I've tried ChatGPT for drafts. The writing sounds like ChatGPT. Our readers notice. I stopped using it after two weeks." — Deputy Editor, Entrepreneurs

 
The third quote is the one that most directly shapes our product positioning. Forbes' readers are financially sophisticated, often wealthy, and attuned to quality. The editorial voice — direct, authoritative, slightly impatient — is a brand asset. Any AI layer that dilutes it creates reputational risk that outweighs the efficiency gain

3. The "AI Content" Question
This section exists because skepticism is legitimate. At least two major newsrooms — CNET and Sports Illustrated — published AI-generated articles in 2023–2024 without adequate disclosure and suffered significant reader trust damage. We need to address this directly, not footnote it.
 
3.1  Why We Are Not Building an Article Writer
The internal debate about whether to include article drafting in Quill's scope was resolved by a single data point: when we showed Forbes readers (n=200, online panel, February 2026) two articles on the same topic — one written by a Forbes journalist, one generated by GPT-4 with Forbes-style prompting — 71% correctly identified the AI article. The most common description of the AI piece was "technically correct but not insightful."
 
Forbes' competitive advantage is not speed. It's the quality of judgment embedded in reporting. AI cannot yet replicate the source relationships, contextual instincts, and narrative intelligence that make Forbes journalism worth paying for (or worth advertisers' money). An article drafted by AI, even heavily edited, carries a trust deficit that is hard to quantify but real.
 
More practically: if Quill generates article drafts, we will face a newsroom culture problem that derails adoption. Journalists do not want to edit AI copy. They want tools that handle the grunt work and leave the writing to them. We are building the product journalists will actually use.

4.2  System Architecture Overview
 
Quill Data Flow (Simplified)
1. JOURNALIST INPUT — Angle, draft, or article URL submitted via CMS plugin or standalone web UI
2. CONTEXT RETRIEVAL — RAG layer queries Forbes archive, licensed wire feeds (AP, Reuters), and approved web sources; returns top-N relevant chunks
3. PROMPT CONSTRUCTION — Feature-specific prompt template assembled with: style guide injection + retrieved context + journalist input
4. MODEL INFERENCE — Fine-tuned Claude / GPT-4o generates structured output
5. SAFETY FILTER — Output scanned for: factual assertions requiring verification, named individuals, legal risk flags
6. HUMAN REVIEW — Output presented in CMS or Quill UI for editor review, edit, and one-click approval
7. AUDIT LOG — All outputs, edits, and approvals logged for model improvement and editorial accountability


5. Maintaining Forbes Brand Voice
This is the section where most AI content tools cut corners, and where we cannot. Forbes' editorial voice is the product. Getting this right is more important than any feature on the roadmap.
